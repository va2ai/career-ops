# Cold Outreach DM Templates -- Chris Combs

Voice/Applied AI targets. All variants under 250 chars. Lead with a concrete signal of fit, then the ask, then a one-line "why reply." No corporate-speak. No comp talk. No weakness signaling.

---

## 1. Base Template (reusable)

> Hey {first_name} -- saw {specific_signal_about_company}. I shipped a real-time Twilio + Gemini Live agent (pure-TS audio codec, 30 voices, tool calling) and a 25-tool MCP platform on GCP. Open to chat about {role_or_team}? Happy to send the repo + a 60-sec demo.

**Slots:** `{first_name}` `{specific_signal_about_company}` `{role_or_team}`
**Char budget:** ~245 chars when filled.

## 3. Three Closes (swap the last line of any DM)

**Cool / professional**
> Worth a 15-min intro call this week or next?

**Warm / curious**
> Even if there's nothing open, I'd love to hear how you're thinking about {their_problem_area} -- happy to trade notes.

**Direct / transactional**
> If there's a fit, I can send CV, repo links, and a 60-sec demo today. If not, no worries -- a pointer to the right person would help.

---

## Usage Notes

- **Personalize the signal**, not the closer. Recipients smell template clones; they expect a clean close.
- **Best signals (in order):** a recent launch / changelog / talk / blog post they shipped > a tech detail Chris has shipped that maps to their stack > a mutual connection or shared community.
- **Drop the demo offer** if the recipient is non-technical (recruiter, COS, founder doing biz). Keep the repo for engineers.
- **Follow up once at 5-7 days** if no reply. Single line: "Bumping this in case it got buried -- happy to make it easy if there's any interest."
- **Never send two DMs to the same company in the same week.** Pick the best-fit recipient (hiring manager > eng lead > IC on the team > recruiter).
- **Source signals from:** their LinkedIn posts (last 30 days), company changelog, eng blog, recent podcast/conference talk, GitHub releases, X/Twitter.
